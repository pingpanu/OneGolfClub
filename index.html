Golf History

Golf's origins are generally traced back to the 15th century in Scotland. The game quickly gained popularity, leading to the establishment of various golf clubs and the formalization of rules. Early golf was often played with rudimentary equipment, but over centuries, the sport evolved into the sophisticated game we know today, with advanced equipment, intricate course designs, and global tournaments. Key milestones include the invention of the gutta-percha ball in the mid-19th century and the steel shaft in the early 20th century, both significantly impacting the game's playability and reach. Major championships like The Open Championship, Masters Tournament, U.S. Open, and PGA Championship are testament to golf's enduring appeal and rich history.

The game's development is also marked by the contributions of legendary players, course architects, and innovators who continually pushed the boundaries of the sport. From the links courses of Scotland to the lush, manicured greens across the world, golf continues to captivate millions, blending athleticism, strategy, and respect for tradition.

Golf Glossary

Birdie

A score of one stroke under par on a hole.

Bogey

A score of one stroke over par on a hole.

Eagle

A score of two strokes under par on a hole.

Fore!

A warning shouted when there is a danger of hitting other players or spectators with a golf ball.

Handicap

A numerical measure of a golfer's ability, used to allow players of different skill levels to compete against each other fairly.

Par

The predetermined number of strokes that a skilled golfer should take to complete a hole, or a round.

Putt

A shot played on the green with a putter, intended to roll the ball into the hole.

Rough

The areas of natural, unmowed grass and other obstacles on a golf course, typically alongside the fairway.
